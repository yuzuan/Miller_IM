# Figure 2 legend

**Figure 2 | The Miller-IM program preferentially maps to MCG1/MCG2-like states in an independent cross-sectional myeloid atlas.**

**A,** Patient-level state-versus-rest Miller-IM score differences across nine author-defined states in 21 primary IDH-wild-type glioblastomas. MCG1 showed the strongest positive difference (mean +0.279; 17/18 evaluable patients; FDR 6.87 × 10−5), followed by MCG2 (+0.098; 16/20; FDR 0.00406); MDSC-like states were lower than the remaining states. The mean MCG3 score differed in direction from the raw-count pseudobulk sensitivity analysis, so no stable MCG3 conclusion was drawn. **B,** Patient-balanced heat map of 19 measurable Miller-IM genes and non-overlapping author marker scores used for state-label quality control. Final label-defining markers had no direct overlap with Miller-IM genes. GSE278456 is cross-sectional; it localizes the program but does not test recurrence.
